## Experience Pillars by User Phase

### Host Acquisition Funnel

- **Travelers**
  - Before: Encounter a compelling “Become a host” story, signals of community trust, and an easy waitlist entry point.
  - During: Receive transparent status updates and human check-ins so they know where they stand in the host review pipeline.
  - After: Get thoughtful feedback even if declined, plus invitations to keep engaging or refer friends who fit better.
- **Hosts**
  - Before: Understand Mayhouse’s ethos, obligations, and benefits via landing page, eligibility checklist, and FAQ hub.
  - During: Complete a guided onboarding covering story, logistics, media, identity verification, and payout setup; preview their draft experience.
  - After: Use an approval dashboard to see curator notes, publish-ready status, and access a resource library for storytelling and safety best practices.

### Experience Data Backbone

- **Travelers**
  - Before: Browse richly detailed, verified experiences with tags, schedules, capacity cues, and trust badges.
  - During: Receive real-time notifications about changes, secure confirmations, itineraries, reminders, and a direct support channel.
  - After: Leave reviews, capture memories, claim proof-of-attendance tokens, and escalate issues through a responsive resolution flow.
- **Hosts**
  - Before: Fill out structured templates for narrative, itinerary, pricing, and assets to keep submissions consistent.
  - During: Manage calendar and capacity, track automated notifications, and preserve edit history while flagging issues to curators.
  - After: Review analytics on attendance and conversion, manage testimonials, toggle embargo or archival states, and iterate on templates with historical context.

### Marketplace Surfaces

- **Travelers**
  - Before: Explore curated collections and themed discovery, see availability clarity, and join waitlists when bookings are full.
  - During: Use day-of tools—maps, messaging, emergency contacts—while enjoying immersive storytelling on experience pages.
  - After: Respond to review nudges, share keepsakes or highlights, earn loyalty credits, and get recommendations for related hosts.
- **Hosts**
  - Before: Control listing visibility in their dashboard, polish storytelling blocks, and stage experiences before going live.
  - During: Monitor live rosters, message attendees, manage incidents, and capture attendance data in real time.
  - After: Send thank-you notes or perks, follow post-event playbooks, resolve disputes quickly, and apply insights to future runs.
